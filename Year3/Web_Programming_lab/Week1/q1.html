Whisper in the Woods-By Robert Frost

The trees stand tall in quiet grace,
A hush of winter on their face.
The snow, like dust, has settled deep,
And in the shadows, creatures sleep.

A path unworn, a distant trail,
Where footfalls fade and winds prevail.
The branches stretch, both soft and bare,
To speak of things that wander there.

The brook has frozen, still and wide,
Its murmurs lost beneath the tide.
Yet in the silence, I still here,
A whisper only I come near.

It calls me on, beyond the gate,
Where roads divide and hearts await.
The road I take is not the same,
But all the woods, they call my name.
